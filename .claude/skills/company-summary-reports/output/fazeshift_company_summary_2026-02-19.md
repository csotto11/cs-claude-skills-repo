## Mission & Vision

> "Empower accounts receivable to drive impact."

Fazeshift believes that "real accounts receivable automation should simply work" — freeing finance teams from hours of manual, error-prone work so they can focus on strategic decisions. The long-term ambition is to bring the most powerful AI to finance and accounting teams worldwide, starting with the end-to-end AR lifecycle.

---

## Core Products & Services

Fazeshift ships a suite of specialized AI agents, each targeting a distinct step in the accounts receivable workflow:

### Cash Application Agent
Matches incoming payments to open invoices, including complex parent-child account hierarchies, partial payments, and multi-invoice matching scenarios that stump rules-based systems.

### Collections Agent
Automates dunning — personalized, brand-aligned payment reminders and escalation sequences — based on customer payment behavior patterns.

### Contract Review Agent
Reads sales contracts and validates them against actual billing; handles usage-based and variable billing automatically.

### Billing Agent
Converts sales contracts into subscriptions or invoices and pushes them into billing systems (Stripe, NetSuite, Sage Intacct) without manual re-entry.

### Credit Agent
Pulls credit reports and automatically updates credit limits or places holds in the ERP.

### Payment Dispute Agent
Monitors customer AP portals, tracks invoice status, and responds to disputes autonomously.

### Payment Portal & Onboarding Agent
Self-serve customer interface for invoice viewing and payment; onboarding agent collects billing details via forms that sync directly with ERP systems.

## Competitive Differentiators

1. **Handles the hard stuff** — Unlike older AR tools that automate only straightforward invoices, Fazeshift explicitly targets complex edge cases: parent-child payment hierarchies, variable billing, contract-to-invoice conversion. This is the work that still requires human AR staff at most companies.
2. **Agentic architecture** — Rather than a monolithic AR platform, Fazeshift ships discrete AI agents that slot into existing ERP/billing stacks (NetSuite, SAP, Sage Intacct, Stripe, Salesforce). Customers don't rip and replace — they augment.
3. **Deep finance domain expertise in the founding team** — CEO Caitlin Leksana (BCG consultant, Harvard MBA) and CTO Timmy Galvin (MIT CS + MBA, 7-year US Navy nuclear submarine officer) bring an unusually credible combination of operational and engineering rigor to a domain that is notoriously complex.
4. **Speed and efficiency signal** — 20-30% monthly ARR growth with a 10-person team and 30+ customers is a strong early indicator of product-market fit before a large go-to-market investment.

## Strategic Risks & Challenges

1. **Platform risk from ERP incumbents** — NetSuite, SAP, and Sage Intacct could build native AI AR modules, reducing the need for a point solution like Fazeshift. The company's edge must outpace what the ERP vendors ship.
2. **Niche TAM ceiling** — AR automation is a genuine problem but a narrower market than horizontal finance platforms. Growth beyond a certain point may require expanding into AP, cash management, or adjacent workflows.
3. **Enterprise sales cycle and implementation complexity** — Landing and expanding within enterprise accounts takes time and CS investment. At 10 people, capacity to support complex deployments at scale is limited until the team grows.
